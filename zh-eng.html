Hao Zhang

Academic Profile

Hao Zhang is a faculty member at the School of Electronic and Optical Engineering, Nanjing University of Posts and Telecommunications. His research focuses on plasmonic hot electrons, ultrafast spectroscopy, inorganic perovskite devices, and AI-assisted scientific research, with continued interest in interdisciplinary advances across optoelectronic materials and device physics.

Research Areas

Current Directions

Plasmonic Hot Electrons

Studying plasmon excitation, hot-electron generation, and interfacial energy transport mechanisms.

Ultrafast Spectroscopy

Using time-resolved ultrafast measurements to probe excited-state evolution, carrier dynamics, and energy relaxation processes.

Inorganic Perovskite Devices

Focusing on thin-film engineering, structural design, and performance optimization of inorganic perovskite optoelectronic devices.

AI-Assisted Research

Exploring AI-supported workflows for information synthesis, experimental analysis, and academic writing assistance.
